# Publication strategy: graphene nanoplate + covellite CuS

## Is this already published?
A closely related experimental paper appears to already exist:
- *Reinforcement in thermoelectric properties of graphene nanoplatelets integrated covellite CuS nanocomposites for green heat harvesting applications* (Journal of Alloys and Compounds, 2025; ScienceDirect index: `S0925838825070446`).

Related CuS interface/surface electronic-structure studies also exist:
- *Electrical Contacts to MoS2 with Covellite (CuS): The Contact Type and Schottky Barrier Modulation* (ACS Omega, 2026).
- *A theoretical investigation of the (0001) covellite surfaces* (older DFT surface study).

## Recommended novelty pivot for high-impact publication
To avoid overlap with the 2025 thermoelectric composite paper, pivot to one of the following:

1. **Interfacial charge-transfer engineering for toxic-ion capture (Pb2+/Cd2+) under realistic aqueous conditions**
   - Include explicit solvation and ionic strength effects.
   - Compute charge-density differences + Bader + work-function shifts + adsorption free energies.

2. **Defect- and termination-resolved CuS(001)/graphene heterointerface atlas**
   - Compare Cu-terminated, S-terminated, and mixed reconstructions.
   - Quantify band alignment, Schottky barrier trends, and transport proxies.

3. **Multi-scale validation paper**
   - DFT (GPAW/QE) + Boltzmann transport + experimental benchmarking.
   - Build reproducibility package: structures, raw trajectories, convergence tables, publication-ready figures.

## What to report for journal-quality rigor
- Convergence tables: cutoff, k-point, slab thickness, vacuum size.
- Error bars/uncertainty from parameter sweeps.
- Full reproducibility metadata: code version, pseudopotentials, runtime hardware.
- Compare results against the 2025 GNP-CuS thermoelectric study and clearly state what is *new* (adsorption/charge-transfer/defect physics, not only bulk thermoelectric trends).
